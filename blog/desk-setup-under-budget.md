## Organization Tips

### Cable Management Solutions

Route cables along the back edge of your desk using adhesive cable channels. This hides them from view while keeping them accessible.

For standing desks, cable rails or trays keep wires organized and prevent them from dangling when you adjust height.

**Quick cable management checklist:**
- Bundle unused cables with Velcro straps
- Route charging cables to specific spots with clips
- Use a cable sleeve for multiple cables running together
- Keep one accessible USB hub for easy device connections

### Drawer Organizers

If your desk has drawers, add small organizers to create designated spots for accessories. Keep a drawer specifically for cables, another for earbuds, and a third for miscellaneous items.

This prevents desk clutter while keeping everything within arm's reach.

## Ergonomics for Your Desk Setup

### Phone Stand Height and Angle

Position your phone stand so the screen is at eye level when you're sitting upright. This prevents neck strain from looking down.

Adjustable stands let you fine-tune the angle for your specific setup. The goal is maintaining neutral neck posture throughout your workday.

### Viewing Distance

Keep your phone roughly arm's length away when viewing content. This reduces eye strain and encourages better posture.

A phone stand at the correct height achieves this naturally.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I really need a phone stand?

Yes. A phone stand improves posture, keeps your phone visible, and frees your hands for other tasks. The small investment pays off in daily convenience.

### What's the most important cable management item?

Cable clips are the highest-impact item. They prevent cables from falling, keep them organized, and make your desk feel tidy with minimal effort.

### Can I build a good desk setup gradually?

Absolutely. Start with a phone stand and cable clips. Add wireless charging next, then invest in better organization solutions as your budget allows.

### How do I prevent cables from tangling?

Use cable clips to keep charging cables in fixed positions. When not in use, bundle cables with Velcro straps. Avoid leaving cables loose on the desk surface.
