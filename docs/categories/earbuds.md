## What to Look For

**Sound quality** — Look for balanced drivers (10mm+ for deeper bass). Check for AAC or LDAC codec support for better wireless audio.

**Battery life** — TWS earbuds typically offer 5-8 hours per charge, with the case adding 20-30 hours total.

**Active Noise Cancellation (ANC)** — ANC blocks background noise for focused listening. Essential for commutes and noisy environments.

**Water resistance** — IPX4 handles sweat and light rain. IPX5+ is better for intense workouts.

**Comfort and fit** — Multiple ear tip sizes and a secure fit matter for long listening sessions. TWS with ear hooks suit active use.

**Call quality** — Multi-microphone arrays with ENC (Environmental Noise Cancellation) improve call clarity.

**Latency** — Important for gaming and video. Lower latency (under 100ms) provides better sync.

## FAQ

**Are expensive TWS earbuds worth it?**
Premium earbuds offer better ANC, sound quality, and features. Mid-range options (₹2000-5000) often hit the sweet spot for everyday users.

**How long do TWS earbuds last?**
With proper care, 2-3 years is typical. Battery degradation is the main limiting factor — replaceable earbuds are emerging.

**Do neckband earbuds still make sense?**
Yes. They're harder to lose, have longer battery life, and cost less than TWS with similar sound quality.

**What is ENC vs ANC?**
ENC (Environmental Noise Cancellation) removes background noise during calls. ANC (Active Noise Cancellation) reduces ambient sound for the listener.

**Are wired earphones still relevant?**
Absolutely. For pure sound quality, wired earphones under ₹1000 often outperform wireless options at double the price.
