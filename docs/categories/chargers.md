## What to Look For

**Power output (watts)** — Higher wattage means faster charging, but your device must support the fast charge standard. Check your phone's max charging speed before buying.

**GaN vs traditional** — Gallium Nitride (GaN) chargers are smaller, cooler, and more efficient than silicon-based chargers. They cost a bit more but are worth it for desk setups.

**Port count** — Single-port chargers are compact. Multi-port chargers let you charge phone + tablet + earbuds from one plug.

**Cable compatibility** — USB-C to USB-C is the modern standard. Make sure your cable supports the same power output as your charger.

**Safety certifications** — Look for BIS (India) or CE/FCC marks. Uncertified chargers can damage devices or overheat.

## FAQ

**Does a higher-wattage charger damage my phone?**
No. Modern phones draw only the power they need. A 65W charger won't fry a phone that only accepts 20W.

**Is GaN worth the extra cost?**
Yes, especially for desktop chargers. GaN runs cooler and fits more ports in a smaller body.

**What's the difference between fast charging and rapid charging?**
Fast charging typically refers to 18W-30W USB-C Power Delivery. Rapid charging is a loosely used term — check actual wattage for real comparisons.

**Should I buy a car charger with both USB-A and USB-C?**
If you have older and newer devices, a dual-port car charger covers both. USB-C is the future, but USB-A cables are still common.
