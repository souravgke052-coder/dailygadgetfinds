## What to Look For

**Actual capacity vs rated** — Many budget power banks deliver 60-70% of rated capacity. Stick to trusted brands with verified performance.

**Input/output ports** — USB-C with Power Delivery (PD) is the fastest. Look for 18W-30W output for quick phone charging.

**Pass-through charging** — Allows you to charge the power bank while it charges your devices. Useful for overnight use.

**Form factor** — Slim 10000mAh fits in pockets. Larger capacity banks are better for backpacks.

**LED indicator** — Accurate battery percentage display helps you plan recharges.

**Air travel rules** — Power banks up to 10000mAh are generally allowed in cabin luggage. Above 27000mAh may be restricted.

## FAQ

**How many times can a 20000mAh power bank charge my phone?**
Most smartphones have 4000-5000mAh batteries. A real-world 20000mAh power bank delivers roughly 4-5 full charges after conversion losses.

**Is 10000mAh enough for a day trip?**
Yes. For most users, 10000mAh covers a full day of moderate phone use without recharging the power bank itself.

**Should I get a power bank with wireless charging?**
Wireless power banks are convenient for Qi-compatible phones but are slower and less efficient. They work best as a secondary charger.

**What's the safest capacity for flying?**
Power banks must be in cabin luggage (never checked in). Most airlines allow up to 10000mAh without approval, and up to 27000mAh with airline permission.
